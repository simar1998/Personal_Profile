About Me

I'm Simar Pal Kalsi. I began coding with Visual Basic in grade 8 and later joined the FRC team 1310 RUNNYMEDE ROBOTICS in high school, mastering Java and developing vision-detection robots and mobile logging apps connected to MYSQL databases. Skilled in C++, Java, C#, and Python, I initiated a pilot program at Mech Solution Ltd during my first year at York University, culminating in the launch of Cloud 3D Print. As its CTO for 2 years, I developed backend systems, optimized video streaming, and facilitated partnerships with companies like Kodak and Creality. I also secured a US patent for a 3D communication method and garnered NRC IRAP program support. After resuming my undergrad studies during the pandemic, I interned with Professor Rueben Moller at Emily Carr University, aiding in image analysis projects. My latest work is 'Sculpt Path' in C++, enhancing 3D printing capabilities by analyzing triangulated meshes to allow for non plannar toolpath motion. I wish to get my private pilot's liscnece one day and buy a Cessna 172 Skyhawk.
